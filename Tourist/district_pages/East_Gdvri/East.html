East Godavari

Nestled between the Eastern Ghats and the shimmering coastline of the Bay of Bengal, East Godavari is a breathtaking blend of sacred sanctuaries, lush forests, cascading waterfalls, and rich cultural heritage. Often hailed as the "God's Own District" of Andhra Pradesh, it is home to some of India’s most revered temples like the Draksharamam Bhimeswara Temple a Pancharama Kshetra, the grand Annavaram Satyanarayana Swamy Temple and the mystically sculpted idol at Ryali Jaganmohini Kesava Temple . These timeless shrines echo spiritual tranquility, drawing pilgrims from across the nation. For nature lovers and adventure seekers, East Godavari unfolds like a paradise. The emerald rainforests of Maredumilli and Rampachodavaram cradle hidden gems such as Rampa Jalatarangini and Amruthadhara waterfalls along with panoramic viewpoints like Ettikallu and Sokuleru Vagu offering soul-stirring vistas of the Eastern Ghats. Wildlife thrives in the serene Maredumilli Sanctuary and the mangrove-rich Coringa Wildlife Sanctuary the latter being India’s second-largest stretch of mangroves and a haven for rare bird species. The majestic Godavari River flowing gracefully through Rajahmundry defines the land—its beauty accentuated by engineering marvels like the Dowleswaram Barrage and the iconic Havelock Bridge . Along the coast, Hope Island and NTR Beach in Kakinada invite peace seekers and sun chasers alike. From the floral wonders of Kadiam Nurseries to the spiritual charm of Talupulamma Lova the coastal allure of Antarvedi and the eco-adventure of Papikonda Hills the district offers experiences as diverse as its landscape. Whether you're tracing the footsteps of ancient sages, trekking through misty forests, exploring heritage museums like that of Sir Arthur Cotton or simply cruising on the serene waters of the Godavari, East Godavari promises an unforgettable journey—where every corner whispers stories of grandeur, grace, and timeless beauty.

Temples

Draksharamam Bhimeshwara and Manikyamba Temple


Draksharamam Bhimeshwara Temple, located in the East Godavari district of Andhra Pradesh, is one of the most sacred Pancharama Kshetras, dedicated to Lord Shiva. Revered as Sri Bhimeswara Swamy Temple, this architectural marvel is believed to have been constructed during the reign of the Eastern Chalukyas in the 9th century CE, with significant additions made by the Cholas and the Kakatiyas. The temple is an exceptional blend of spiritual heritage and Dravidian architecture, featuring massive granite structures, intricately carved pillars, and a unique two-storey sanctum (Garbha Griha) housing both the Shiva Lingam and Goddess Manikyamba Devi.

Annavaram Satyanarayana Temple


Located on Ratnagiri Hills, Annavaram Satyanarayana Swamy Temple is a revered Vaishnavite shrine dedicated to Lord Veera Venkata Satyanarayana, a form of Vishnu. Famous for its Satyanarayana Vratham, the temple draws lakhs of devotees, especially during Ekadashi and festivals. With serene views of the Pampa River, a three-tiered sanctum symbolizing the trinity, and easy access via road or steps (~460), the temple blends devotion with natural beauty and spiritual fulfillment.

Korukonda Narasimha Temple


The Korukonda Lakshmi Narasimha Swamy Temple in East Godavari, Andhra Pradesh, is an ancient Vaishnava Divya Kshetra primarily located atop a steep hill. This "wishing hill" is home to a unique, 9-inch Swayambhu (self-manifested) Lord Lakshmi Narasimha Swamy idol, accessible via 615 steps. Believed to be 700-800 years old, the temple features intricate Ramayana carvings and strictly follows Agama Sastra rituals. Its historical significance, combined with the challenging climb and panoramic views, makes it a revered pilgrimage site, especially during major festivals like the Kalyana Mahotsavam.

Ryali Jaganmohini Keshava Temple


The Ryali Jaganmohini Temple in Konaseema, Andhra Pradesh, is renowned for its unique 11th-century black Salagrama stone idol, which depicts Lord Vishnu (Kesava Swamy) from the front and the enchanting Mohini from the rear. This extraordinary 5-foot tall, dual-sided deity is a marvel of ancient sculpture, representing the legend of Vishnu's Mohini avatar during the Samudra Manthan. The temple also features continuous water flow from the deity's feet (Akasha Ganga) and intricate carvings of the Dashavataras. Opposite, the Uma Kamandaleswara Swamy Temple houses a Shiva Lingam consecrated by Lord Brahma. This rare complex, where Vishnu and Shiva temples face each other, holds deep mythological and spiritual significance.

Bikkavolu Vinayaka Temple


The Bikkavolu Vinayaka Temple, or Sri Lakshmi Ganapathi Temple, in East Godavari, is an ancient shrine built by the Eastern Chalukyas around the 9th century. Its main attraction is a magnificent 7 to 11-foot tall, monolithic, "Swayambhu" Lord Ganesha idol, uniquely featuring its trunk turned to the right. Devotees believe that whispering wishes into the Lord's ear here leads to their fulfillment. The temple, part of a complex showcasing Eastern Chalukyan architecture, is also known for the belief that the idol is continuously growing and for a mysterious water flow from its base.

Kotasattemma Temple


The Kotasattemma Temple, located in Nidadavole of West Godavari district, Andhra Pradesh, is a deeply revered shrine dedicated to Goddess Sattemma, considered a fierce and protective local manifestation of Shakti (Durga/Parvati). The temple is especially popular among devotees who seek blessings for childbirth, health, family welfare, and protection from evil forces. The presiding deity, Kotasattemma Ammavaru, is portrayed in a powerful seated form, richly adorned with traditional ornaments and silk attire, evoking both maternal compassion and divine authority.

Kumararamam


The Kumaramam Bhimeswara Temple in Samarlakota is a revered 9th-10th century Pancharama Kshetra dedicated to Lord Shiva, built by King Chalukya Bhima I. It houses a towering 16-foot tall white Shiva Lingam (Spatika Linga), believed to be a fragment of a Lingam shattered by Lord Kartikeya. Devotees can worship the Lingam from both ground and first floors. The temple features impressive Eastern Chalukyan architecture with 100-pillared mandapams and attracts pilgrims, particularly during Maha Shivaratri, for its spiritual significance and historical grandeur.

Antarvedi Lakshmi Narasimha temple


The Antarvedi Lakshmi Narasimha Temple, located at the sacred confluence of the Vashista Godavari River and the Bay of Bengal in Konaseema, Andhra Pradesh, is a significant pilgrimage site. Believed to be built between the 15th-16th centuries, it's dedicated to Lord Lakshmi Narasimha, whose idol uniquely faces west. The temple showcases impressive Dravidian architecture with intricate carvings and a five-story gopuram. Legends attribute its sanctity to ancient yagnas and Lord Narasimha's victory over a demon. Its serene "Sagara Sangamam" location and the grand annual Kalyanotsavam festival attract numerous devotees seeking spiritual blessings.

Pitapuram Kukkuteswara and Puruhuthika Devi temple


The Pithapuram Kukkuteswara and Puruhuthika Temple complex in Kakinada district is a highly significant pilgrimage site, known as "Pada Gaya Kshetram" for ancestral rites. It features a unique Swayambhu Shiva Lingam, Lord Kukkuteswara Swamy, resembling a rooster's head. Within the same complex is the shrine of Goddess Puruhuthika Devi, one of the Ashtadasha Shakti Peethas where Sati's body part is believed to have fallen. The temple boasts impressive Dravidian architecture, a large single-stone Nandi, and a sacred pond, making it a confluence of Shaiva and Shakta traditions with other shrines, including Sri Pada Vallabha.

Talupulamma Lova


Talupulamma Lova Temple, located in a scenic valley near Tuni in East Godavari, Andhra Pradesh, is a highly popular shrine dedicated to Goddess Talupulamma. It is renowned for the widespread belief that the Goddess protects new vehicles from accidents, drawing thousands of owners who bring their vehicles for blessings. Set amidst lush hills and forests, the temple offers a tranquil atmosphere and is particularly vibrant on weekends and during festivals like Dasara, attracting devotees seeking safe journeys and blessings for their new acquisitions.

Ainavilli Vinayaka temple


Ainavilli Sri Vigneswara Swamy Temple, the deity who removes obstacles and grants the first worship, is a prominent self-manifested temple located in the Konaseema region of East Godavari district. Here, the Lord appeared as a self-manifested (Swayambhu) deity from a single stone during the Krita Yuga. This temple is considered to be older than the Kanipakam temple. The Vinayaka here fulfills the desires of devotees and is regarded as a precious gem by them. It is said that Daksha’s Yagna (sacrifice) was disrupted because he did not worship the Lord at this temple before performing the Yagna. Therefore, devotees firmly believe that any auspicious event undertaken after worshiping the Lord of Ainavilli will surely be successful. According to the Sthala Purana, it is believed that Sage Vyasa first installed the idol of Vinayaka here before embarking on his journey to South India.

Appanapalli Bala Balaji Temple


The Appanapalli Balaji Temple, or Sri Bala Balaji Devasthanam, in Konaseema district, Andhra Pradesh, is a highly revered shrine dedicated to Lord Venkateswara in his "Bala Balaji" (child god) form, often called "Bala Tirupati." Situated picturesquely on the banks of the Vynateya River, it was established by a devotee, Sri Molleti Rama Swamy, following a divine vision. The temple, consecrated in 1991, is particularly noted for its Nithya Annadanam (daily free meal scheme), a pioneering initiative in the region. Its gopuram features intricate sculptures, and major festivals like Brahmosthavams draw large numbers of devotees.

Vadapalli Venkateswara Temple.


The Vadapalli Venkateswara Temple in Konaseema, Andhra Pradesh, is a highly revered shrine dedicated to Lord Venkateswara, uniquely famous for its presiding deity that is observed to be 'sweating' continuously, a phenomenon considered miraculous by devotees. Located on the banks of the sacred Gautami River, this ancient temple features traditional South Indian architecture. Pilgrims believe that worshipping here offers benefits equivalent to visiting Tirumala. The temple attracts thousands, particularly during major festivals, drawn by its spiritual power and the extraordinary nature of the deity.

ISKCON Rajahmundry


The ISKCON Temple in Rajahmundry, formally known as the Sri Sri Radha Gopinath Temple, is a beautifully constructed and spiritually uplifting center for Gaudiya Vaishnavism, dedicated to Lord Krishna (Gopinath) and Sri Radha. Situated on the picturesque banks of the Godavari River, this temple is one of the largest ISKCON temples in South India and a major center of the International Society for Krishna Consciousness (ISKCON) in Andhra Pradesh. The temple was inaugurated in the early 2000s under the guidance of ISKCON leaders and visionaries like HH Bhakti Swarup Damodar Swami and has since become a spiritual hub for Krishna devotees. Architecturally, the temple combines modern aesthetics with traditional temple design.

Nature and Scenic Views

Maredumilli


Maredumilli is situated amidst the breathtaking Eastern Ghats, approximately 90 km from Rajahmundry. This region is characterized by its semi-evergreen forests, diverse flora and fauna, and a pleasant climate, especially during the post-monsoon and winter months (October to March). The area is managed as an eco-tourism project, focusing on sustainable tourism that benefits local tribal communities. Key attractions include several picturesque waterfalls, such as Jalatharangini Waterfalls (a multi-tiered cascade accessible via a short trek) and Amruthadhara Falls (another beautiful seasonal waterfall, offering a more adventurous trek).

Rampa Falls


Rampa Falls, also called Rampachodavaram Falls, is a beautiful 50-foot waterfall located near Rampachodavaram village in Andhra Pradesh's Alluri Sitharama Raju district. Surrounded by dense semi-evergreen forests, it offers a refreshing escape and typically has water year-round. Accessible via a thrilling jeep ride and a short 20-minute trek, visitors can enjoy shallow pools for bathing. The site also features the ancient Sri Neelakanteswara Temple, where tribal dances are performed during Shivaratri. It's a popular spot for nature lovers, especially between October and February.

Jalatarangini Falls


Jalatharangini Waterfalls, located near Maredumilli in Andhra Pradesh, is a popular natural attraction known for its unique multi-tiered cascades, resembling a "staircase of water." Unlike single-drop falls, its gentle flow over various rock formations creates shallow, accessible pools, perfect for wading and enjoying the cool, clear water. Surrounded by lush semi-evergreen forests, it offers a serene and picturesque setting. Best visited during and after the monsoon (July-November) for optimal water flow, Jalatharangini is ideal for families, picnicking, and photography.

Manyam View Point


Manyam Viewpoint, located in the scenic Maredumilli forest range of Alluri Sitharama Raju district (formerly part of East Godavari), is one of the most captivating and lesser-known hill viewpoints in Andhra Pradesh. Perched atop the Eastern Ghats at a high elevation, this viewpoint offers panoramic views of endless green valleys, misty hill ranges, and dense forests that stretch to the horizon. It is a peaceful retreat where visitors can truly disconnect from urban life and soak in the untouched beauty of the Godavari uplands. Situated just a short drive from the Maredumilli Bhadrachalam road, Manyam Viewpoint is part of the broader eco-tourism circuit developed by the Forest Department with the involvement of local tribal cooperatives.

Sokuleru View Point


Vanjangi, a rising hill station near Visakhapatnam, is famously known as "Cloud Heaven" for its spectacular "cloud ocean" phenomenon, which blankets the valleys below the viewpoints, especially during winter mornings. This magical sight, where mountain peaks emerge like islands from a sea of clouds, attracts numerous tourists and photographers. Located in the picturesque Eastern Ghats, Vanjangi offers a tranquil escape and breathtaking panoramic views. Visitors typically arrive early to witness the stunning sunrise over the cloud-filled landscape, making it an unforgettable natural wonder.

Gudisa Hill Station


Gudisa Hill Station, nestled near Chintapalle in the Alluri Sitharama Raju district, is a remote and mesmerizing hilltop destination in the Eastern Ghats that offers breathtaking 360° views of misty mountains, endless green meadows, forest valleys, and the untouched tribal heartland of Andhra Pradesh. Still largely undiscovered by mainstream tourism, Gudisa is a dream location for campers, off-road bikers, photographers, and trekking enthusiasts. Located at an elevation of around 3,300 feet (1,000 meters) above sea level, Gudisa is part of the hill ranges that connect Lambasingi, Vanjangi, and Paderu, and lies around 12–15 km from Chintapalle town. The ascent to Gudisa involves driving or riding through steep ghat roads, forest paths, and scenic villages, making it as adventurous as it is rewarding. The final stretch often requires 4x4 vehicles or bikes, and the thrill of reaching the top adds to the excitement.

Papikondalu hills


Papikondalu, or "Papi Hills," are a breathtaking hill range in Andhra Pradesh's Eastern Ghats, famously traversed by the Godavari River forming a deep, scenic gorge. Renowned for its panoramic boat cruises, which start from towns like Rajahmundry, the journey showcases towering hills, dense tropical rainforests, and a serene riverine landscape. This protected National Park offers stops at the Gandi Pochamma Temple and Perantapalli Village, with options for unique overnight stays in riverside bamboo huts. Often compared to Kashmir for its beauty, Papikondalu provides a tranquil escape and is best visited between October and March.

Papikondalu Park


Papikonda National Park, notified in 2008, is home to rich biodiversity, including tigers, leopards, wild dogs, gaurs, sambar, spotted deer, sloth bears, and over 100 species of birds and butterflies. River cruises operated from Rajahmundry, Polavaram, or Kolluru offer visitors an immersive journey through the heart of the region. Many cruises stop at tribal villages and spiritual places like Perantapalli Ashram, Gandi Pochamma Temple, and Vruksha Vruksha Mandir. Camping, eco-stays, bamboo huts, and riverbank resorts have been developed along the route, especially near Kolluru, offering a peaceful overnight stay experience under the stars. Tribal dishes, including bamboo chicken and country-grown millet meals, add to the flavor of the visit. The best time to explore Papikondalu is October to February, when the river is calm, the hills are green, and the air is cool and misty.

Amruthadhara Waterfalls


Amruthadhara Falls, located about 15 km from Maredumilli in Andhra Pradesh's Alluri Sitharama Raju district, is a beautiful seasonal waterfall cascading in two stages amidst dense forests. Best visited post-monsoon (August-September), it requires a 1 km steep trek to reach. While not ideal for bathing, its serene beauty and refreshing mist make it a popular spot for nature lovers and photographers. Local tribes maintain the access path, and folklore suggests the "nectar-like" waters possess healing properties.

Pallam Island


Pallam Island, located near Konaseema in the Godavari River delta of Andhra Pradesh, is a serene and relatively untouched river island that offers visitors a unique blend of natural beauty, rural charm, and peaceful seclusion. Nestled between the branching distributaries of the mighty Godavari River, this island is accessible primarily by ferry or small motorboats from towns like Yanam, Injaram, or Muramalla, making the journey as enchanting as the destination. The island is a perfect example of deltaic riverine beauty—lush with green paddy fields, coconut groves, areca nut plantations, and winding canals. It’s a place where time seems to slow down. The soft rustle of palm trees, gentle lapping of river water, chirping of birds, and the distant chants from village temples together create a peaceful and immersive experience.

Pora Island


Pora Island," more accurately referred to as Pora Village, is a secluded and fascinating settlement nestled deep within the intricate network of mangrove forests in the Katrenikona Mandal of Dr. B. R. Ambedkar Konaseema District, Andhra Pradesh. This remote village is essentially an island, entirely surrounded by water bodies, creeks, and the dense, ecologically rich mangrove cover that characterizes the Godavari delta. Life in Pora Village offers a glimpse into a unique way of living, largely disconnected from modern urban conveniences, where the residents' daily lives are intimately intertwined with the surrounding waterways and forest ecosystem. Access to Pora is primarily by boat, navigating through narrow channels amidst the sprawling mangrove roots, making the journey itself an adventurous experience. The village is known for its serene, untouched natural beauty, offering a tranquil escape for those interested in exploring untouched rural landscapes and observing local traditions

Konaseema Region


The Konaseema region in Andhra Pradesh is a "God's Own Creation," renowned for its captivating beauty shaped by the Godavari River's delta. It features an intricate network of backwaters and canals, perfect for boat cruises, bordered by endless lush green paddy fields and towering coconut groves. Significant mangrove forests like Coringa Wildlife Sanctuary add to its ecological richness. The confluence of the river and sea at Antarvedi offers stunning vistas, complementing the region's serene rural charm, rich cultural traditions, and numerous ancient temples, making it a truly picturesque and spiritually enriching destination.

Coringa Wildlife Sanctuary


The Coringa Wildlife Sanctuary, Andhra Pradesh, is India's second-largest mangrove forest, located in the Godavari estuary near Kakinada. A vital coastal protector, it harbors over 35 mangrove species and diverse fauna, notably the endangered Fishing Cat and being a crucial nesting ground for Olive Ridley sea turtles. Over 120 bird species, including migratory ones, make it a birdwatcher's paradise. Visitors can enjoy boat rides through its intricate creeks or walk on a 600-meter boardwalk for an immersive experience. Best visited from November to March, its ecological significance is underscored by its Ramsar site designation.

Beaches

Antarvedi Beach


Antarvedi Beach, located in Konaseema, Andhra Pradesh, is a peaceful beach at the "Sagara Sangamam", where the Vashista Godavari River meets the Bay of Bengal. Known for its serene atmosphere and golden sands, it provides a tranquil escape and is often visited by pilgrims due to the nearby Antarvedi Lakshmi Narasimha Swamy Temple. The beach offers beautiful sunrise and sunset views and a glimpse into local fishing traditions, best enjoyed during the cooler months (October-March).

Uppada Beach


Uppada Beach, located near Kakinada in the Kakinada district of Andhra Pradesh, is a quiet, wide, and pristine stretch of coastline known for its soft golden sands, gentle waves, and peaceful atmosphere. Unlike more commercial beaches, Uppada offers a calm and less crowded experience, making it a favorite among locals, nature lovers, and road-trip travelers looking for a scenic getaway by the Bay of Bengal. What sets Uppada Beach apart is its natural expanse and rural charm. The beach is lined with patches of casuarina groves, grassy dunes, and clear open skies. The wide shore and shallow waters make it safe for walking along the tide, light splashing, and watching fishing boats come ashore with the morning catch. It’s a perfect spot for sunrises, as the early golden light reflects beautifully on the sand and sea.

Odalarevu Beach


Odalarevu Beach in Konaseema, Andhra Pradesh, is a serene and pristine coastal retreat known for its clean golden sands and calm, shallow waters. Unlike bustling tourist spots, it offers a tranquil environment perfect for relaxation, leisurely walks, and enjoying spectacular sunrises and sunsets. Located amidst Konaseema's lush landscape, where Godavari tributaries meet the Bay of Bengal, this beach provides a peaceful escape and a glimpse into traditional coastal life, ideal for nature lovers seeking quiet beauty.

NTR Beach


NTR Beach, located near Kakinada in the Kakinada district of Andhra Pradesh, is a quiet and serene beach developed in honor of the legendary actor and former Chief Minister Nandamuri Taraka Rama Rao (NTR). Situated around 12 km from Kakinada city, close to the village of Vakalapudi, this beach offers a calm, family-friendly coastal experience away from heavy tourist traffic. The beach features a long shoreline of soft golden sand, flanked by stretches of casuarina groves and gentle waves of the Bay of Bengal. It has been developed as a recreational beach park, with support from the Andhra Pradesh tourism department. Visitors can enjoy neatly paved walkways, shaded benches, children's play areas, and scenic viewpoints, making it ideal for morning walks, picnics, and relaxed evenings with family and friends.

Tourist Locations

Godavari Bridge, Rajahmundry


The Godavari Bridge at Rajahmundry (also known as the Kovvur–Rajahmundry Bridge) is not just a transportation structure — it's a landmark of engineering brilliance, colonial history, and regional pride. Stretching majestically across the mighty Godavari River, this bridge connects the East and West Godavari districts and serves as a vital link in the cultural and economic fabric of Andhra Pradesh. Built in 1900 by the British, it was one of the earliest major rail bridges in India. Though now decommissioned, its rustic iron trusses still stand as a heritage monument, admired for their strength and elegance.

Dowleswaram Barrage


The Dowleswaram Barrage, officially the Sir Arthur Cotton Barrage, is a historic and vital irrigation project built across the Godavari River near Rajahmundry. Originally constructed by British engineer Sir Arthur Cotton in 1852 and later rebuilt between 1970-1982, this 3.6-kilometer-long barrage transformed the Godavari Delta from a flood-prone, drought-stricken area into a fertile agricultural powerhouse. It controls floods and irrigates millions of acres annually. The barrage, along with the Sir Arthur Cotton Museum, serves as a significant tourist attraction, offering scenic views and a testament to pioneering engineering that brought prosperity to the region.

Arthur Cotton Museum


The Sir Arthur Cotton Museum, located in Rajahmundry near the Dowleswaram Barrage, is a unique museum dedicated to Sir Arthur Thomas Cotton, the British engineer who revolutionized irrigation in Andhra Pradesh. Housed in a colonial-era building close to the banks of the Godavari River, the museum offers an in-depth look into the engineering marvels, visionary projects, and life of Sir Cotton, who is often revered in the region as a savior of farmers and godlike figure for his transformative work. Opened in 1988 by the Andhra Pradesh Irrigation Department, the museum features a wide array of historical exhibits, models, maps, engineering drawings, and photographs. These displays illustrate how Cotton implemented canal systems, water regulation structures, and ultimately built the Dowleswaram Barrage in 1850 — which turned the barren lands of East and West Godavari into fertile fields, forever changing the fate of the region.

Festivals

Prabhala Teertham


The Prabhala festival, also known as Prabhala Theertham, is a vibrant Andhra Pradesh festival celebrated during Sankranti, particularly in the Konaseema region. It's characterized by the procession of elaborately decorated, towering structures called Prabhalu, which are carried by villagers through fields and canals. These Prabhalu, often resembling temple structures, house idols of deities, primarily Lord Shiva, and are a symbol of community devotion and cultural pride.
