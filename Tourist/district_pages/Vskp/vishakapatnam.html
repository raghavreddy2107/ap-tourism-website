Vishakapatnam

Visakhapatnam district, often referred to as the "Jewel of the East Coast," is a captivating blend of coastal charm, verdant hills, ancient heritage, and spiritual sanctity. Nestled between the Eastern Ghats and the Bay of Bengal, this region offers an enchanting array of experiences—from the serene, sun-kissed shores of Rushikonda , Yarada, and Bheemili Beaches, to the cool, mist-laden hill stations of Araku Valley, Lambasingi and Paderu. Nature lovers are drawn to the cascading Katiki, Kothapally and Devarapalli Waterfalls while trekkers and sunrise seekers flock to the fog-wrapped trails of Vanjangi Hills. The district is also steeped in spiritual richness with revered temples like Simhachalam, Appikonda Shiva Temple and the mystic Devipuram offering both divine peace and architectural marvels. Cultural heritage comes alive in the Borra Caves , Thotlakonda Buddhist Complex and the colonial traces in Bheemunipatnam while museums like the INS Kurusura Submarine and TU-142 Aircraft Museum narrate India's naval and military pride. The lively festivals of Sirimanotsavam and Sambara Jathara reflect the deep-rooted traditions of the region's diverse communities.With its lush forests, exotic wildlife, sacred shrines, pristine lakes, and a perfect blend of history, culture, and adventure, Visakhapatnam district is not just a tourist destination—it's a soulful journey through the heart of Andhra Pradesh’s natural and cultural splendor.

Temples

Simhachalam Temple


Simhachalam Temple in Visakhapatnam is a historic and sacred Hindu shrine dedicated to Lord Varaha Narasimha, a rare avatar of Vishnu. Situated atop a scenic hill, the temple is renowned for its unique tradition of covering the deity with sandalwood paste year-round, removed only during the grand Chandanotsavam festival. Built in a fusion of Kalinga and Dravidian architectural styles, the temple boasts elaborately carved pillars and rich history dating back to the Eastern Ganga and Vijayanagara dynasties. Surrounded by lush hills, it is both a pilgrimage hub and a cultural gem of Andhra Pradesh.

Sri Kanaka Mahalakshmi Temple


Sri Kanaka Mahalakshmi Temple, located in the old city of Visakhapatnam, is a revered shrine dedicated to the city's presiding deity, Goddess Kanaka Mahalakshmi. Known for its open-air sanctum and constant flow of devotees, the temple is deeply rooted in local faith and tradition. Believed to have miraculous origins, the idol is worshipped especially on Thursdays, drawing large crowds. Simple yet spiritually powerful, the temple plays a central role in the religious life of Vizag, especially during the Margasira month when grand celebrations are held in honor of the Goddess.

Appikonda Someswara Temple


The Sri Someswara Swamy Temple at Appikonda, about 30 km south of Visakhapatnam, is a 11th-century Chola-built shrine dedicated to Lord Shiva. Legend credits sage Kapila Maharshi with manifesting sacred lingams on the hill, giving the village its name, and today four “swayambhu” lingams remain enshrined. The site features an impressive monolithic Nandi statue and partly buried shrines, set against the tranquil Bay of Bengal. A protected monument, it's famed for its Maha Shivaratri celebrations, drawing massive crowds in a devotional blend of history, myth, and natural beauty.

Rushikonda Venkateswara Temple


The Sri Venkateswara Swamy Temple on Rushikonda hill is a modern yet traditional coastal shrine managed by TTD, built from 2018 and consecrated in March 2022. Spread over 10 acres with shrines to Hanuman, Sridevi, and Bhudevi, it mirrors the Tirupati temple's style, featuring masterful stone carvings. Easily accessible via a 500 m hill road or footpath, it draws thousands daily, offering panoramic views of Rushikonda Beach and a calm spiritual atmosphere by the sea.

Sri Sampath Vinayaka Temple


Sri Sampath Vinayaka Temple in Asilmetta, Vizag, is a black-marble shrine to Lord Ganesha built in 1962 to address architectural doshas. It gained fame when Admiral Krishnan broke 1,001 coconuts here in 1971 following the sinking of the PNS Ghazi. Known for its vehicle blessings, exam-ticket rituals, and Tamil-style worship, it holds daily homams and elaborate Ganesh Chaturthi festivities. Open morning (6–10:30 AM) and evening (5:30–8:30 PM) with free darshan, this spiritual hub welcomes students, drivers, fishermen, and families seeking blessings for success, safety, and prosperity..

Devipuram


Devipuram is a serene Shakta temple complex near Visakhapatnam, featuring the remarkable three-story Sahasrakshi Meru Temple—a walkable 3D Sri Chakra structure built between 1985 and 1994 by ex‑nuclear scientist Guruji Sri Amritananda Natha. Dedicated to Goddess Sahasrakshi, the complex hosts over 100 yogini statues and allows devotees of any background to perform rituals themselves. It’s also a spiritual learning center and social outreach hub through the Sri Vidya Trust.

Kailasagiri Shiva Statue


The Kailasagiri Shiva–Parvati statues are a striking 40-foot divine ensemble at the centre of Visakhapatnam's hilltop park. Erected on a lush hill overlooking the Bay of Bengal, they embody tranquility and spirituality. Reached by ropeway or scenic road, the statues form a spiritual highlight amid gardens, viewpoints like Titanic Viewpoint, a floral clock, toy train, and Shanti Ashram, blending devotion with natural beauty.

Hill Stations

Araku Valley


Araku Valley, nestled in the Eastern Ghats about 120 km from Visakhapatnam, is a serene hill station known for its lush coffee plantations, cool climate, and rich tribal heritage. Surrounded by misty hills, waterfalls, and thick forests, it offers scenic train rides through tunnels and bridges, especially beautiful during monsoons and winter. Attractions like Padmapuram Gardens, Chaparai Waterfalls, the Tribal Museum, and Galikonda Viewpoint add to its charm. With its peaceful vibe, natural beauty, and cultural depth, Araku is a perfect escape for nature lovers, trekkers, and anyone seeking a refreshing retreat.

Lambasingi


Lambasingi, a charming village in Andhra Pradesh's Visakhapatnam district, is famously known as "Andhra Kashmir" due to its remarkably cold climate and occasional frost or near-freezing temperatures in winter, a unique phenomenon for South India. Perched in the Eastern Ghats at 1,000 meters, it offers breathtaking natural beauty with dense forests, coffee plantations, and serene spots like Thajangi Reservoir and Kothapalli Waterfalls. Its distinct weather and picturesque landscapes make it a popular and tranquil getaway for those seeking a cool escape and stunning views.

Paderu Hill Station


Paderu is a charming hill station nestled in the Eastern Ghats of Andhra Pradesh, known for its cool climate, lush green landscapes, and deep-rooted tribal culture, residing at over 900 meters elevation. It serves as an administrative hub and a gateway to exploring the region's serene natural beauty, offering opportunities for nature walks amidst dense forests and hill streams. While less commercialized, Paderu provides an authentic experience of tribal life and is a convenient base for visiting nearby attractions like the ancient Borra Caves and the scenic Chaparai Waterfalls. Its tranquil ambiance makes it a perfect retreat for those desiring an immersive natural and cultural experience.

Ananthagiri


Ananthagiri Hill Station, positioned in the Eastern Ghats on the route to Araku Valley, is a refreshing getaway known for its cool climate and extensive coffee plantations, which produce famous organic coffee. This scenic locale boasts numerous viewpoints offering panoramic vistas of the surrounding hills and valleys. Key attractions also include the ancient Borra Caves nearby and the Lord Anantha Padmanabha Swamy Temple, a site of historical and spiritual significance. With its lush greenery, serene ambiance, and opportunities for nature exploration, Ananthagiri provides a perfect blend of natural beauty and cultural interest.

Vanjangi


Vanjangi, a rising hill station near Visakhapatnam, is famously known as "Cloud Heaven" for its spectacular "cloud ocean" phenomenon, which blankets the valleys below the viewpoints, especially during winter mornings. This magical sight, where mountain peaks emerge like islands from a sea of clouds, attracts numerous tourists and photographers. Located in the picturesque Eastern Ghats, Vanjangi offers a tranquil escape and breathtaking panoramic views. Visitors typically arrive early to witness the stunning sunrise over the cloud-filled landscape, making it an unforgettable natural wonder.

Waterfalls

Katiki Waterfalls


Katiki Waterfalls, a stunning natural cascade fed by the Gosthani River, is situated deep within the Eastern Ghats, near the renowned Borra Caves in Andhra Pradesh. Plunging from about 50 feet, it offers a picturesque sight and a refreshing pool at its base. Reaching the falls involves an adventurous journey, typically a jeep ride followed by a scenic 2-kilometer trek through rocky and forested terrain, where local "Bamboo Chicken" is a popular snack. Its pristine setting and the engaging trek make it a highly sought-after destination for nature lovers and adventurers visiting the Araku Valley region.

Thatiguda Waterfalls


Thatiguda Waterfalls, also known as Tadimada or Ananthagiri Waterfalls, is a beautiful 100-foot cascade located near Ananthagiri in the Araku Valley region, fed by the Gosthani River. It's distinguished by its multi-tiered flow amidst lush greenery and towering rocks. Accessible by a scenic drive and a short walk through local villages, the falls offer refreshing waters for bathing and photography. Being a perennial waterfall, it's beautiful year-round, but most captivating during and after the monsoon season, providing a serene natural escape.

Devarapalli Waterfalls


Devarapalli Waterfalls—also known as Sariya or Bahubali Falls—is a seasonal, ~49 ft cascade about 85 km from Vizag, set in a dense Eastern Ghats forest. Access involves a rugged 15–18 km road drive followed by a 2–3 km trek with stream crossings and rocky climbs. The natural pool beneath is perfect for a chilly dip and the waterfall offers hidden viewpoints and even small caves. Best visited during winter and post-monsoon, it's open around 6 AM–6 PM with no entry fee, light refreshments available, and requires sturdy shoes, water, and extra caution.

Chaparai Waterfalls


Chaparai Waterfalls, also known as Dumbriguda Waterfalls, is a popular and accessible natural attraction located near Araku Valley. It's unique for its natural water slides formed by water flowing over smooth, sloped rock formations, making it an engaging spot for recreation and family fun. Surrounded by dense forests, the area provides a picturesque picnic setting. Its ease of access and refreshing atmosphere, along with the availability of local delicacies like "Bamboo Chicken," make Chaparai a favored destination for visitors seeking both natural beauty and an enjoyable outdoor experience in the region.

Ranajilleda Waterfalls


Ranajilleda Waterfalls is a beautiful and serene natural cascade located in the remote Eastern Ghats of Visakhapatnam district, Andhra Pradesh. This pristine waterfall offers a captivating sight amidst lush greenery and is accessible via an adventurous trek through dense forests. Its cool, clear pool at the base provides a refreshing experience. Ideal for nature lovers and trekkers, Ranajilleda is best visited during or after the monsoon for its full majestic flow, providing a tranquil escape from urban life.

Kothapally Waterfalls


Kothapally Waterfalls is a beautiful, multi-tiered cascade roughly 60–100 ft high, located deep in the Eastern Ghats near Gangaraju Madugula village. A 2 km forest trek leads to the base, where the Gosthani River flows over rock shelves into a natural pool—set amidst coffee and strawberry farms. Open daily (6 AM–5:30 PM) with ₹10 entry, it shines post‑monsoon to winter (Oct–Mar). Facilities include steps, changing rooms, and snacks; ideal for families, photographers, birders, and trekkers seeking an affordable, peaceful nature retreat.

Matsyagundam Waterfalls


Matsyagundam, meaning "fish pool," is a unique and sacred destination in the Araku Valley, famous for a natural pool abundant with tame, revered fish. Located approximately 115 km from Visakhapatnam, it's believed these fish were saved by a "Mother Fish" and are protected by Lord Sri Matsyalingeswara Swamy, whose century-old temple stands nearby. This site is significant for its local folklore and vibrant Maha Shivaratri celebrations, offering a blend of natural wonder, spiritual tranquility, and rich cultural heritage where visitors can feed and interact with the fish.

Beaches

Ramakrishna(RK) Beach


RK Beach, or Ramakrishna Beach, is Visakhapatnam’s bustling 3.7 km coastal promenade on the Bay of Bengal. Lined with parks, sculptures, and clean golden sands, it offers everything from water sports (jet ski, paddle boat), camel/horse rides, beach volleyball, kite-flying, to lively street food scenes—all free to enter and open 5 AM–midnight. Highlights include the INS Kursura Submarine Museum, Kali Temple, Visakha Museum, and Victory-at-Sea Memorial. Enhanced by Eco-Vizag efforts with lifeguards and cleanliness drives, it’s a prime spot for families, sunset chasers, morning walkers, and culture lovers .

Rushikonda Beach


Rushikonda Beach, a "Jewel of the East Coast" near Visakhapatnam, is celebrated for its pristine golden sands, clear blue waters, and scenic hills, offering a perfect blend of relaxation and adventure. It's a major hub for diverse water sports including jet skiing, parasailing, and scuba diving, catering to thrill-seekers and casual visitors alike. Beyond activities, the beach is ideal for swimming, sunbathing, and enjoying the beautiful coastal views. Its well-maintained environment and proximity to other tourist spots make Rushikonda a highly popular and appealing destination for all types of visitors.

Yarada Beach


Yarada Beach is a tranquil coastal gem around 15 km from Vizag, framed by green hills and a scenic ghat drive. Known for its golden sands, peaceful vibe, and postcard-worthy sunrises and sunsets, it’s quieter than RK or Rushikonda. Amenities are minimal—just some huts and coconut stalls—and while swimming isn't safe due to strong currents, it's perfect for relaxation and photography. The nearby lighthouse (~₹10) offers stunning hilltop views. Open 24/7 with no entry fee, it’s best visited between November and February.

Bheemili Beach


Bheemili Beach, or Bheemunipatnam Beach, near Visakhapatnam, is a tranquil and historically significant coastal spot where the Gosthani River meets the Bay of Bengal. Known for its calm waters, golden sands, and less crowded ambiance, it's ideal for swimming and relaxation. The beach is rich in history, featuring 17th-century Dutch fort remnants, the Hollanders Green cemetery, and an ancient Lord Narasimha Temple. Its scenic beauty, historical landmarks, and statues along the promenade offer a unique blend of nature, heritage, and peace, making it a captivating destination.

Mangamaripeta Beach


Mangamaripeta Beach, located about 9 km from Visakhapatnam, is a serene and pristine coastal gem known for its calm waters and golden sands. Its standout feature is a remarkable natural rock arch (or sea bridge), a unique geological formation carved by the sea, providing a stunning photographic backdrop. Offering a quieter alternative to city beaches, Mangamaripeta is ideal for peaceful swims, relaxation, and has also become popular for kayaking. With its untouched beauty and unique natural arch, it's a perfect spot for nature lovers and those seeking tranquility away from crowds.

Lawson Bay


Lawson's Bay Beach, also known as Pedawaltair Beach, is a tranquil and pristine extension of Visakhapatnam's coastline, known for its whitish sands and clear, shallow waters, making it one of the safest beaches in Vizag for swimming and sunbathing. Located near RK Beach, it offers a serene escape with lush green surroundings and beautiful sunrise/sunset views. Its calm environment also makes it suitable for beginner surfing and leisurely walks, appealing to those seeking peace and natural beauty away from the crowds.

Sagarnagar Beach


Sagarnagar Beach in Visakhapatnam is a serene and uncrowded coastal gem, celebrated for its golden-white sands, clear waters, and tranquil atmosphere. It offers a peaceful escape, ideal for leisurely strolls and breathtaking sunsets. While less commercialized, it's gaining popularity for adventure activities like paragliding and boating. Its pristine beauty, enhanced by recent beautification efforts, provides a calm and picturesque setting perfect for relaxation and photography, away from the city's hustle.

Appikonda Beach


Appikonda Beach is a tranquil, uncrowded golden‑sand beach about 30 km south of Vizag, flanked by greenery and rocky hill terrain. With calm, clear waters ideal for swimming or snorkeling, it's perfect for sunrises, solitude, and simple beach cricket. Adjacent stands the 11th‑century Someswara Swamy Temple—built in Chola times—celebrated during Maha Shivaratri. There’s no entry fee and few amenities, so come prepared, and consider hiking up the hill for stunning coastal views.

Parks and Museums

Kailasagiri


Kailasagiri Park is Visakhapatnam's iconic hilltop destination, offering stunning panoramic views of the Bay of Bengal and the city from its 173-meter elevation. Its key attractions include a towering 40-foot Shiva-Parvati statue, a grand Floral Clock, and a unique Titanic Viewpoint. The park provides a thrilling Ropeway ride to the summit, a delightful Toy Train, and a Children's Play Park, making it suitable for all ages. With opportunities for paragliding, serene walks, and access to the World Telugu Museum, Kailasagiri blends natural beauty with entertainment and cultural significance.

INS Kursura Submarine Museum


The INS Kursura Submarine Museum on Visakhapatnam's RK Beach is Asia's first and the world's second submarine museum, housed in the decommissioned Soviet-built INS Kursura (S20). After 31 years of distinguished service, including the 1971 Indo-Pak War, this 91.3-meter submarine was ingeniously moved ashore and opened to the public in 2002. Visitors can explore its authentic compartments like the control room, torpedo room, and crew quarters, gaining a rare insight into submarine life and naval history. Guided by ex-naval personnel, it offers a unique, educational, and patriotic experience.

TU 142 Aircraft Museum


The TU 142 Aircraft Museum in Visakhapatnam proudly showcases a decommissioned Soviet-built Tupolev Tu-142M long-range maritime reconnaissance and anti-submarine warfare aircraft, which served the Indian Navy for 29 years. Located on Beach Road, it allows visitors to explore the aircraft's interior, viewing original equipment like anti-submarine missiles and the cockpit. An exhibition hall, VR flight simulators, and guided tours enhance the experience. This museum is a significant attraction, offering a unique glimpse into India's naval aviation history and technology.

Vishaka Museum


The Visakha Museum, located on RK Beach Road in Visakhapatnam, is a public museum housed in a colonial Dutch bungalow, dedicated to preserving and showcasing the region's rich historical, cultural, and maritime heritage. Inaugurated by the Indian Navy, its diverse collection includes ancient artifacts, maritime exhibits with ship models and naval instruments, traditional arts, and colonial-era remnants. The museum offers a comprehensive insight into Visakhapatnam's past, its evolution as a port city, and its cultural significance, making it a valuable educational and historical attraction.

Tribal Museum , Araku


The Tribal Museum in Araku Valley, established in 1996 by APTDC, is a unique two-story museum dedicated to showcasing the vibrant culture and daily life of the nearly 19 indigenous tribes of the Eastern Ghats. Built with traditional mud and metal, it features life-sized dioramas, authentic tribal artwork, and exhibits of daily tools, jewelry, and musical instruments. A highlight includes displays of Mayur and Dhimsa tribal dances. The museum also houses a Tribal Art and Crafts Centre where visitors can buy local handicrafts, offering an immersive educational and cultural experience.

Matsyadarshini Aquarium


Matsyadarshini Aquarium, located on RK Beach in Visakhapatnam, is a popular destination showcasing a diverse collection of marine and freshwater species. Established in 1994, it houses exotic fish and invertebrates in well-maintained tanks. The aquarium is an educational hub, promoting marine life conservation. Visitors can enjoy interactive exhibits and purchase souvenirs. It's open daily from 9 AM to 9 PM.

VUDA Park


VUDA Park, or Dr. Y.S. Rajasekhara Reddy Central Park, is a large urban park in Visakhapatnam, offering a wide range of recreational activities and scenic beauty. Key attractions include a musical fountain, a skating rink, and water sports facilities. The park's lush gardens are ideal for relaxation, and there's a dedicated play area for children. Known for hosting cultural events, VUDA Park is a popular destination for both locals and tourists.

Nature Spots and Sanctuaries

Borra Caves


Borra Caves are vast limestone caverns in Araku Valley, 705 m above sea level and about 80 m deep. Carved over millions of years, they feature dazzling stalactite and stalagmite formations—including a natural Shivalinga. Discovered in 1807, these caves also contain ancient Paleolithic tools. Lit paths wind about 200 m through chambers named after their iconic shapes. Cooling to ~18 °C, the site’s entrance temple commemorates a mythic cow rescue. Best visited Oct–Mar on your scenic Araku route with other waterfalls nearby.

Dolphin's Nose Viewpoint


Dolphin's Nose is a uniquely shaped hill in Visakhapatnam, resembling a dolphin's snout, offering stunning panoramic views of the city, coastline, and Visakhapatnam Port from its 358-meter elevation. A historic lighthouse atop the hill serves as a crucial navigational aid. The viewpoint provides breathtaking vistas, especially during sunrise and sunset. Located nearby is the serene Yarada Beach. This landmark is a popular destination for its scenic beauty and tranquil atmosphere.

Thotlakonda Buddhist Complex


Thotlakonda Buddhist Complex, near Visakhapatnam, is an ancient monastic site on a hilltop overlooking the Bay of Bengal, active from the 2nd century BCE to 2nd century CE. Discovered in 1976, it was a significant hub for Buddhist monks and missionaries along ancient maritime trade routes. The complex features a Mahastupa, votive stupas, Chaitya-griha (prayer hall), and Viharas (monastic cells), alongside evidence of daily monastic life. Archaeological finds include ancient coins and artifacts. Its serene location and historical importance make it a profound site for understanding early Buddhism in India.

Bavikonda Buddhist Site


Bavikonda is an ancient Buddhist monastery complex ~16 km from Vizag, atop a rain‑water‑harvesting hill. Dating back to 300 BCE–200 CE, its brick remains include a grand stupa, monastery cells, chaitya halls, and monks’ quarters. Excavations unearthed Buddha relics, Roman and Satavahana coins, pottery, inscriptions, and hundreds of votive stupas. Open 9 AM–5 PM, free entry, it’s part of the coastal Buddhist trail with Thotlakonda and Pavuralakonda—ideal for history lovers and hikers.

Kondakarla Ava lake and Sanctuary


Kondakarla Ava Lake and Sanctuary, located about 50 km from Visakhapatnam, is Andhra Pradesh's second-largest natural freshwater lake and a significant bird sanctuary. Covering over 635 hectares, it's a haven for over 123 bird species, including migratory and resident waterfowl like painted storks and jacanas. The lake also supports rich aquatic flora, various fish species, and even smooth-coated otters. Conservation efforts are ongoing to declare it Andhra Pradesh's first 'Conservation Reserve'. Visitors can enjoy birdwatching and traditional boat rides, making it an ideal tranquil escape for nature lovers, especially from November to February.

Kambalakonda Wildlife Sanctuary


Kambalakonda Wildlife Sanctuary, a significant green lung for Visakhapatnam, is a sprawling forest reserve spanning over 70 sq km, characterized by dry evergreen forests and hilly terrain. It's home to diverse wildlife, including rare species like the Indian leopard, barking deer, wild boar, and a variety of birds and reptiles. Beyond wildlife spotting, the sanctuary offers numerous eco-tourism activities such as trekking, night safaris, birdwatching, and adventure sports like zip-lining and kayaking. With its natural beauty and range of activities, Kambalakonda provides a perfect blend of wilderness exploration and outdoor adventure.
