Srikakulam

Srikakulam District, located in the northeastern part of Andhra Pradesh, is a region rich in history, culture, and natural beauty. Bordered by Odisha to the north and the Bay of Bengal to the south, the district boasts a diverse landscape that includes coastal areas, lush green hills, and fertile plains. Historically, Srikakulam was part of the Kalinga Empire and later came under the rule of the Eastern Ganga dynasty, leaving behind a legacy of remarkable architecture and cultural heritage. The district is home to several ancient temples, including the Arasavalli Sun Temple, dedicated to Lord Suryanarayana, which is believed to have been constructed in the 7th century and showcases stunning Kalinga-style architecture. Another significant temple is the Srikurmam Temple, dedicated to Lord Vishnu in his Kurma (tortoise) avatar, making it a unique religious site. The Srimukhalingam Temple, built during the Vajrahasta ruling period, is an architectural marvel reflecting the grandeur of medieval temple construction. Srikakulam also has a strong Buddhist influence, with sites like Salihundam, which features ancient stupas and relics from the Buddhist era. The district’s coastline, stretching over 193 kilometers, is dotted with serene beaches such as Kallepalli Beach and Baruva Beach, offering breathtaking views of the Bay of Bengal. Forts like Dannanapeta Megalithic Site, which dates back to the Iron Age, provide a glimpse into the region’s prehistoric past. The Telukunchi Bird Sanctuary is a haven for bird watchers, hosting migratory birds in a tranquil setting. With its blend of historical significance, spiritual landmarks, and natural beauty, Srikakulam District stands as a fascinating destination for travelers and history enthusiasts alike.

Temples

Arasavalli Suryanarayana Temple


Located in Srikakulam, Andhra Pradesh, is one of the oldest sun temples in India. Dedicated to Lord Surya, the temple is believed to have been built in the 7th century CE by King Devendra Varma of the Eastern Ganga Dynasty. The temple is renowned for its architectural brilliance, designed in the Rekha deula style of Kalinga architecture, similar to the famous Jagannath Temple in Puri1. A unique feature of the temple is that the early morning sun rays directly fall on the deity's feet twice a year, even when the entrance gates are closed.

Kurmanatha Swamy Temple


Kurmanatha Swamy Temple, located in Srikurmam, Andhra Pradesh, is a unique Hindu temple dedicated to Lord Vishnu in his Kurma (tortoise) avatar. It is the only known pre-14th-century temple where Vishnu is worshipped in this rare form. Originally a Shaivite shrine, it was transformed into a Vaishnavite temple by Anantavarman Chodaganga Deva of the Eastern Ganga Dynasty in the 11th century. The temple showcases a blend of Kalinga and Dravidian architecture, featuring intricate murals and 108 distinct stone pillars. A fascinating aspect of the temple is its tortoise park, honoring the deity’s form.

Mukhalingeswara Swamy Temple


Mukhalingeswara Swamy Temple, located in Srimukhalingam, Andhra Pradesh, is a significant Hindu temple dedicated to Lord Shiva. It is renowned for its exquisite architecture and intricate carvings, reflecting the grandeur of the Eastern Ganga dynasty. The temple complex features a large central shrine with a towering shikhara (spire) and numerous smaller shrines surrounding it. The temple is also known for its unique sculptures depicting various deities and mythological scenes, making it a treasure trove for art and history enthusiasts.

Andhra Vishnu Temple


The Andhra Vishnu Temple, located in Srikakulam village, Krishna District, Andhra Pradesh, is an ancient temple dedicated to Lord Vishnu. It is unique for depicting Vishnu with a Shankha in his right hand and a Chakra in his left, opposite to the usual representation. The temple has existed since the Satavahana era and was later restored by the Rajas of Challapalli. It features over 30 inscriptions, including those from Krishnadevaraya, and is ranked 57th among the 108 Divya Desams. The temple is also associated with King Andhra Vishnu, who is said to have built a massive wall connecting Srisailam, Bheemeshwaram, and Kaleshwaram.

UmaRudra Koteswara Temple


The UmaRudra Koteswara Swamy Temple, located in Srikakulam, Andhra Pradesh, is a revered shrine dedicated to Lord Shiva. It is one of the Pancha Linga Kshetras, five Shiva temples established along the Nagavali River by Lord Balarama. The temple is believed to have been originally built over 5000 years ago, with significant renovations carried out in 1774 by Sri Magatapalli Kamayya Setti. Architecturally, the temple is constructed using Salihundam Red Stone, resembling the South Indian temple style. It holds deep mythological significance, as it is said that Lord Balarama, during his travels, created the Nagavali River to relieve people from drought and established Shiva temples to allow devotees to seek blessings before visiting Sri Kurmanatha Swamy Temple.

Sangam Shiavalayam


Sangam is a village and a pilgrimage site in Vangara mandal in Srikakulam district in the state of Andhra Pradesh, India.Nagavali, Suvarnamukhi and Vegavati rivers confluence (join together) takes place here and hence it is referred as Triveni Sangam. The idol of Lord Sangameswara, another name for Lord Shiva, was here installed (vigraha pratista) by Balaram, the brother of Lord SriKrishna. This is one of the five 'Linga Kshetras'. Thousands of devotees gather here on Maha Shivaratri festival every year.

Forts and Monuments

Mandasa Fort


Mandasa Fort is a historic fort located in the Srikakulam district of Andhra Pradesh, India. Built during the 18th century by the Gajapati rulers, the fort offers a glimpse into the region's rich cultural heritage. The fort is known for its impressive architecture, including its massive stone walls, watchtowers, and intricate carvings. Visitors can explore the various sections of the fort, including the Durbar Hall, prison cells, and temples. The fort also offers panoramic views of the surrounding countryside, making it a popular destination for history enthusiasts and tourists alike.

Salihundam


Salihundam is an ancient Buddhist site located in the Srikakulam district of Andhra Pradesh, India. It features a collection of stupas, monastic cells, and other structures dating back to the 2nd century BCE to the 3rd century CE. The site is known for its well-preserved stupas adorned with intricate carvings and sculptures depicting Buddhist motifs. Salihundam was an important center for Buddhism during its peak and provides valuable insights into the architectural and artistic achievements of ancient Indian civilization.

Beaches

Kalingapatnam Beach


Kalingapatnam Beach is located on the coast of Kalingapatnam, where Vamsadhara River empties into Bay of Bengal. It is located at a distance of 30 km from Srikakulam in Srikakulam district of the Indian state of Andhra Pradesh. Kalingapatnam Beach has been recognized by the State Government, with the state tourism board APTDC promoting it as a tourist destination.As it was an ancient harbor, it is equipped with a lighthouse where exporting of goods such as perfumes and textiles took place, before being closed during British rule.The beach is also known as Open Road Sea, due to the road ending in the sea shore bed

Kaviti


Kaviti village is located at a distance of 130 Kms. from District head quarters in between two towns Viz Sompeta and Ichapuram, towards East. The area constituting Kaviti Mandal is populary known as Uddanam (Udyanavanam). The coastal belt at this place presents a pleasant appearance, covered with large extents of Coconut, Cashewnut, Jack and other fruit trees. The Uddanam area is a scenic spot for the visitors. Two famous temples Chintamani Ammavaru and Sri Sitharamaswamy temple are situated in this Village

Baruva Beach


Baruva Beach is situated on the east coast of Bay of Bengal in Baruva, a village at a distance of 27 kilometres (17 mi) from Palasa.Baruva Beach is one of the oldest beaches in Andhra Pradesh. It was used as seaport until 1948. On the beach is a commemorative pillar placed in remembrance of a cargo ship that sank nearby in 1917.It is also called as the second Goa of India. The famous Sri Kotilingeswara Swamy and Janardhana Swamy temple are located at this place. Further, there is a coconut nursery and a Coir Industry located here. The river Mahendra Tanaya Merges into sea at this place. Thousands of people take seabath during festivals.

Bird Sanctuaries

Teelinaapuram Sanctuary


Telineelapuram is a village located 65 kilometers from SRIKAKULAM & 7 kms from Tekkali in Tekkali mandal,Every year, over 3,000 pelicans and painted storks visit from Siberia to these villages during September and stay on till March. It is a paradise for bird observers. Researchers at Andhra University said the birds had been following this aerial route since 15 years. The migratory movement was first noticed over 15 years ago. At that time the number of birds exceeded 10,000. Today, the figure has been reduced to about 3,000. The Tekkali creek and the villages in its vicinity like Telineelapuram, Ijjuvaram and Naupada in the district have become a seasonal resort for the winged tourists coming from as far as Siberia in Russia, Malaysia, Hungary, Singapore and Germany apart from 113 different species of inland birds, a majority of which are migrants from other states.

Telukunchi Sanctuary


Telukunchi Bird Sanctuary, along with Telineelapuram, is a migratory bird destination in the Srikakulam district of Andhra Pradesh, India. These areas are known for attracting thousands of birds, particularly pelicans and painted storks, from Siberia during the winter months.
